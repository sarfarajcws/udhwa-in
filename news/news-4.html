Heavy Crowd at HP Petrol Pump Udhwa After Price Hike Rumours

March 6, 2026 Udhwa.in Correspondent • 2 min read

Udhwa, March 6: A large crowd gathered at the HP Petrol Pump in Udhwa this morning following rumours of a petrol price hike. Long queues of bikes and other vehicles were seen forming since early morning, causing congestion near the pump.

📢 Rumour Sparks Rush

The rush was triggered by unverified messages circulating on social media, claiming that petrol prices could rise by ₹5 to ₹10 per litre. Fearing the hike, residents rushed to fill fuel.

🏍️ What Was Observed

By the Morning, a significant crowd had gathered at the HP Petrol Pump on the main road. Bike riders were seen waiting in long lines, some for over an hour, to get their tanks filled. The situation remained chaotic for several hours before gradually returning to normal.

The crowd was purely based on the rumours of a price hike. No official announcement regarding any increase in petrol prices had been made.

📢 Official Clarification

Authorities and petrol dealers' associations have since clarified that there is no imminent hike in petrol prices. Official sources confirmed that the country has sufficient fuel stocks for over 25 days, and the rumours circulating on social media are baseless.

✓ No increase in petrol prices has been announced by the government or oil companies.
✓ Adequate fuel stock is available across all pumps in the region.
✓ Residents are urged not to panic and avoid falling for unverified messages.

📅 Timeline of Events at HP Petrol Pump Udhwa

⏰ Early Morning

Social media messages about a price hike began circulating. Small groups started gathering at the pump.

⏰ Peak Rush (9:30 AM)

Long queues stretched onto the main road. Bikes and scooters dominated the crowd. Pump attendants worked overtime to manage the rush.

🗣️ Administration's Appeal

The local administration has requested citizens not to pay heed to such rumours. For official information on fuel prices, always refer to verified sources or the official websites of oil marketing companies. No shortage of petrol exists in Udhwa or the surrounding areas.

"A classic example of how social media rumours can cause real-world chaos. Stay calm and verify before you believe." - Udhwa.in Editorial

Conclusion

The morning rush at the HP Petrol Pump Udhwa serves as a reminder of how quickly misinformation can spread. While the situation is now under control, it highlights the need for residents to rely on official announcements and avoid spreading unconfirmed news. Udhwa.in urges everyone to stay informed, not misled.

⛽ Don't panic. Verify. Stay safe. ⛽
